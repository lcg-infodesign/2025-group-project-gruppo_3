THE GRAPH: THE IMPACT OF THE ERUPTION
The detailed visualization is dedicated to an in-depth analysis of a single volcanic eruption and conveys its overall impact through a clear and readable breakdown of its effects. While the overview allows for quick comparison between different events, this section encourages the viewer to focus on a single episode, highlighting its human, material, and economic consequences.
At the center of the visualization is the impact of the eruption, expressed as the result of the combination of several damage categories. Each element contributes to building a more complete interpretation of the event, transforming numerical data into a narrative of the eruption's intensity and severity.
THE IMPACT SCORE SYSTEM
The impact of each eruption is calculated through an Impact Score, obtained by summing five standardized categories. Each category is evaluated with a score ranging from 1 to 4, where higher values indicate more severe consequences.
The five categories considered are:
  • Damage – Assesses the overall extent of material damage caused by the eruption.
  • Injuries – Measures the number of people injured during the event.
  • Deaths – Indicates the number of fatalities directly attributed to the eruption.
  • Missing – Represents the number of missing or unidentified people following the event.
  • Houses Destroyed – Quantifies the number of residential buildings destroyed.
The sum of the values assigned to each category determines the total impact score of the eruption, making it directly comparable to other events shown in the overview visualization. A higher score corresponds to an eruption with more extensive and severe consequences.
HOW TO READ THE GRAPH
The detailed visualization translates the impact score into a clear and immediate representation of its individual components. Each category is displayed separately, allowing the viewer to understand which aspects of the eruption contributed most significantly to the event's overall severity.
The total score emerges as the final result of the balance between human and material factors, offering a synthetic yet data-driven reading. In this way, the user can move from an analytical view of individual categories to a comprehensive understanding of the eruption as a whole.
ADDITIONAL INFORMATION FOR CONTEXTUALIZING THE ERUPTION
Beyond impact analysis, the detail page provides a set of information that helps contextualize the eruption in time and space. The total number of eruptions recorded for the volcano is indicated, with the possibility to navigate between previous and subsequent events, placing the eruption within the eruptive history of the volcanic system.
When available, the exact date of the eruption is reported, specifying day, month, and year. The visualization also includes a description of the volcano type, accompanied by a background illustration. It is important to note that a volcano's typology is not necessarily fixed: the same volcano may change over time in response to several factors, particularly the composition of its magma.
Finally, a geographic map locates the volcano on a global scale, allowing its position on the planet and the geographical context in which the eruption occurred to be clearly understood.
